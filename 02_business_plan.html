The founder is a trained graphic designer — eliminating the largest recurring cost in apparel. A hybrid print-on-demand/bulk production model keeps inventory risk low while margins improve at scale. 5 Structural Advantages
1. Founder is the designer — $0 external design cost. Most brands pay $300–$800 per design drop.

2. Heat press in-house — enables custom finishing and bulk finishing at lower per-unit cost over time.

3. Hybrid POD/bulk model — POD protects against inventory risk on unproven designs. Bulk only on proven sellers.

4. Austin roots — built-in local creative community, strong independent culture, and a growing premium consumer base.

5. Diversified founder income — Rover + Uber Eats + freelance funds the runway. No desperate decisions under financial pressure.

Know These Before You're In Them
😶
Running Out of Patience Before Money
HIGH
Months 2–4 are the danger zone. Launch energy is gone, sales are slow, content feels pointless. The brand goes quiet — not dramatically, just slowly.
✓ Fix: Pre-commit to Month 3 before you feel like quitting in Month 2. Systems beat motivation. The roadmap exists for this reason.
💸
The Discount Death Spiral
HIGH
Discounting to chase early sales trains your audience to wait for deals. Margins collapse. Once started, very hard to stop.
✓ Fix: Never discount out of anxiety. Use value-adds instead (free sticker, early drop access). Hold your prices.
🎯
Marketing a Product Nobody Wants
MEDIUM
More posts won't fix a product problem. If designs aren't getting saves and DMs, no amount of marketing will convert.
✓ Fix: At Month 3, show your 5 designs to 10 people whose taste you respect. Ask which they'd pay $65 for. Their answer matters more than any metric.
📦
The Inventory Cash Trap
MEDIUM
Moving to bulk too early ties up cash in stock that doesn't move. Can't afford the next drop. Momentum stalls.
✓ Fix: Never order bulk on a design with fewer than 10 POD sales. POD is your safety net — use it longer than feels comfortable.
👻
Brand Drift — Losing Your Voice
MEDIUM
Content gets polished and generic. The emotional connection erodes. Followers stop caring without an obvious reason.
✓ Fix: SBW wins by being more SBW, not less. Once a month — write a post that makes you slightly uncomfortable to publish.
⭐
Quality Problem Left Unaddressed
LOW
One bad batch at premium prices generates reviews that take months to undo.
✓ Fix: Approve every product in your hands before it goes live. Replace every complaint, no questions.

Priority Order
1. Rover first — fastest to money, zero upfront cost.
2. Uber Eats second — fills gaps between Rover and SBW work perfectly.
3. Freelance third — leverages existing design skill.
4. SBW always — building every morning regardless of income status.
Tax Reality
As self-employed across multiple streams, set aside 25–30% of every dollar earned into a dedicated Tax Fund account weekly. Quarterly payments due: Apr 15, Jun 16, Sept 15, Jan 15. Missing payments = IRS penalties that compound.
