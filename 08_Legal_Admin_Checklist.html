Tax
Basics for Wesley

As a self-employed person with multiple income streams, you're responsible for your own taxes. Here's everything you need to know to stay legal and avoid penalties.

The Most Important Rule
Set aside 25–30% of every dollar you earn into a separate savings account labeled "Tax Fund." Do this weekly, automatically, without exception. This covers federal income tax, state income tax (Texas has none — a real advantage), and self-employment tax of 15.3%.

Key Deductions to Track
Business Deductions — Keep Every Receipt
Home Office: If you use part of your apartment exclusively for business, you can deduct a percentage of rent and utilities. Calculate the square footage of your workspace divided by total apartment square footage.

Phone & Internet: The business-use percentage of your phone and internet bill is deductible. If you use your phone 70% for business, deduct 70%.

Adobe Creative Cloud: 100% deductible as a business software expense.

MacBook Pro: 100% deductible as business equipment (Section 179 allows full deduction in year of purchase).

Heat Press: 100% deductible as business equipment.

Lime Scooter Subscription: Deductible as a business transportation expense for Uber Eats delivery.

Packaging & Supplies: 100% deductible as cost of goods sold.

Shopify & App Subscriptions: 100% deductible as business software.

Instagram Ads: 100% deductible as marketing expense.

Mileage: Every business-related trip at 67 cents/mile (2025 IRS rate). Track with MileIQ.
Texas Sales Tax — What You Owe
Texas sales tax rate in Austin: 8.25% (6.25% state + 2% local).

You collect it from customers at checkout (Shopify does this automatically once configured) and remit it to the Texas Comptroller quarterly or annually depending on your sales volume.

Register free at comptroller.texas.gov before your first sale. No registration = personal liability for unpaid sales tax.
comptroller.texas.gov → Sales and Use Tax → Apply for Permit
1099 Forms — What to Expect
Rover, Uber Eats, and any freelance client paying you more than $600/year must send you a 1099-NEC form by January 31. These report your income to the IRS — which means the IRS already knows about this income before you file. Report everything accurately. Keep records of all income even if you don't receive a 1099.
SBW Co. · Legal Guide

Legal
Foundations

The legal setup that protects you, your designs, and your business relationships from day one.

Why the LLC Matters
Without an LLC, you and SBW Co. are legally the same entity. If a customer sues over a product issue, they're suing you personally — which means your personal savings, car, and assets are at risk. The LLC creates a legal wall between Wesley and SBW Co. That wall costs $300 to build and is one of the best investments you'll make.
Trademark vs Copyright — Know the Difference
Trademark protects your brand name and logo — "SBW Co." and the SBW logo. Filed with the USPTO. Prevents others from selling apparel under your brand name. File under Class 25 (clothing). Cost: $250–$350 per class.

Copyright protects your individual graphic designs — the actual artwork on the shirts. Automatically yours when you create it. Formal registration ($65/design at copyright.gov) lets you sue for much larger damages if copied.

You need both. Trademark protects the brand. Copyright protects the art.
Investor Agreement — Non-Negotiable
Before accepting any money from any person — friend, family, or stranger — get a written agreement signed by both parties. It should specify:

• The exact amount being invested
• What they receive in return (equity %, revenue share %, or convertible note)
• Timeline for returns
• What happens if the business fails

Clerky.com has startup-grade legal templates. A simple revenue share agreement can be drafted for under $200 with a lawyer or free with a solid template. The conversation that feels awkward now saves a friendship or family relationship later.
clerky.com · Stripe Atlas · Rocket Lawyer
Store Policies — Required Before First Sale
Shopify requires three policy pages before you can fully activate your store:

Privacy Policy: How you collect and use customer data. Shopify generates this free.
Terms of Service: Rules of using your store. Shopify generates this free.
Refund Policy: What customers can expect if something goes wrong. Write this yourself — be clear and fair.

In Shopify: Settings → Policies → Create from template. Takes 15 minutes.
SBW Co. · Financial Setup

Banking &
Bookkeeping

The financial infrastructure that makes tax time simple and your business look legitimate to investors and suppliers.

The Golden Rule — Never Mix Money
Personal money and business money in the same account is the #1 bookkeeping mistake new founders make. It makes taxes a nightmare, makes your financials unreadable to investors, and in some cases can "pierce the corporate veil" — meaning a court could decide your LLC doesn't actually protect you personally. Separate accounts from Day 1. No exceptions.
Recommended Account Structure
Account 1 — Business Checking (Mercury)
All revenue flows in. All business expenses come out. Mercury is free, has no minimums, and is built for startups. mercuryhq.com. Requires LLC formation documents and EIN.
mercuryhq.com — Free, no minimum balance
Account 2 — Tax Fund (High-Yield Savings)
Transfer 25–30% of every dollar earned weekly. Never touch it except to pay quarterly taxes. Marcus by Goldman Sachs, Ally Bank, or Marcus offer 4–5% APY — your tax money earns interest while it waits. Label it clearly so you're never tempted.
Account 3 — Personal Checking (Existing)
Pay yourself a regular "salary" transfer from the business account monthly — only what the business can afford. This is called an owner's draw for an LLC. Tracking it clearly shows investors that the founder is financially disciplined.
Bookkeeping — Keep It Simple
Wave Accounting (Free)
Connect your Mercury business account and it automatically imports and categorizes transactions. Generates profit & loss statements, tracks expenses by category, and produces reports that a CPA or investor can read. Free forever for the core features. waveapps.com
waveapps.com — Free forever
Monthly Bookkeeping Routine (20 Minutes)
Week 1 of every month:
1. Categorize all transactions from last month in Wave
2. Run a Profit & Loss report — compare to your financial model
3. Transfer this month's tax set-aside to Tax Fund
4. Pay yourself an owner's draw if the business earned it
5. Note any large or unusual expenses for your CPA

That's it. 20 minutes a month keeps your books clean all year.
