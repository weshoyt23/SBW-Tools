Monthly Income Math

Conservative: 2 boarding nights + 8 walks/week = ~$580 gross → ~$465 net/month

Review Strategy

10 Reviews
As Fast As Possible

Reviews are the single most important factor in your Rover success. Here's your complete strategy to hit 10 five-star reviews — and the exact words to use when asking.

⭐ Review Tracker
Tap stars as you earn them
0 of 10 reviews earned
How to Get Each Review
1️⃣
The Discounted First Booking. Offer your first 3–5 bookings at a reduced rate to friends, Nextdoor neighbors, or anyone you can reach directly. Frame it: "I'm building my Rover reviews — would you be open to a discounted first booking in exchange for an honest review?" 70–80% say yes.
2️⃣
Ask in Person, Not Just on the App. When you drop the dog back off, say it out loud: "If you have a second, a Rover review would mean so much to me — it's how new clients find me." Eye contact and a genuine ask converts 3x better than the automated Rover review request email.
3️⃣
The Perfect Follow-Up Text. Send this within 2 hours of drop-off: "Hey! So glad [dog name] had a great time — they were such a joy. If you ever have a spare moment, an honest Rover review would genuinely help me so much. Here's the direct link: [rover link]. No pressure at all!"
4️⃣
Over-Deliver Every Single Time. Send more photo updates than they expect. Have the dog home fed and happy before the agreed time. Leave a note with what their dog ate, how long you walked, their favorite moment of the day. This level of care creates reviews without asking.
5️⃣
The Photo Package. Send 3–5 genuinely beautiful photos from the walk — taken with your photographer's eye. Nobody else does this. A photo package creates an emotional response that almost always leads to a review and a rebook.
6️⃣
Family and Friends First. Ask anyone in your network with a dog to book you on Rover even for a short walk — and leave an honest review. These seed reviews make the profile real for strangers. Even 2–3 reviews from people who know you personally look legitimate and build trust.
7️⃣
Respond to Every Review Publicly. Reply to each review on Rover with a warm, personal thank-you. This shows future clients that you're attentive, engaged, and communicative — exactly what they want in a sitter.

Nextdoor Strategy

Nextdoor
Done Right

Nextdoor is your fastest path to first bookings — warmer than Instagram, more local than Rover search. Here are 5 ready-to-post messages for different moments across the 30 days.

Why Nextdoor Works So Well for This

Rover search shows you to strangers. Nextdoor shows you to neighbors — people who already live near you, walk the same parks, and have a baseline reason to trust you. The conversion rate from Nextdoor to first booking is dramatically higher than any other channel. It should be your first post every time you have something to announce.

Nextdoor Rules to Follow

Never post the same message twice in the same neighborhood — Nextdoor will flag it as spam and hide it.
Post in multiple neighborhoods — yours first, then adjacent ones. Each post is a separate opportunity.
Reply to every comment within minutes. Nextdoor comments rank the post higher and keep it visible longer.
Post on weekday mornings — Tuesday and Wednesday 8–10am gets the most Austin Nextdoor engagement.
Space posts 2 weeks apart per neighborhood so you feel like news, not noise.
