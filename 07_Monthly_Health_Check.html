SBW Co. · Risk Register

Know the
Failure Modes

Every one of these has killed brands like SBW before year two. Knowing them in advance is your protection against them. Read this monthly — what's obvious now becomes easy to miss when you're in it.

😶
Running Out of Patience Before Money
HIGH RISK
The most common killer. Months 2–4 are the danger zone — launch energy is gone, sales are slow, content feels pointless. The brand doesn't die dramatically. It just quietly goes silent.
Early Warning Signs
Posting less than 3x per week · Skipping the monthly health check · Avoiding looking at your Shopify analytics · Telling yourself you'll "get back to it next week"
✓ Fix: Pre-commit to Month 3 before you feel like quitting in Month 2. The roadmap exists for this reason. When motivation disappears, systems are what keep the business alive.
💸
The Discount Death Spiral
HIGH RISK
Discounting to chase early sales trains your audience to wait for deals. Margins collapse. The brand stops feeling premium. Once you start, it's very hard to stop.
Early Warning Signs
Offering discounts in the first 90 days without reviews to justify it · Running sales because you're anxious, not because it's strategic · Multiple discount codes circulating at once
✓ Fix: Hold prices. Use the promo code for new Rover clients only. If you want to incentivize, use value-adds (free sticker, early drop access) instead of price cuts. Never discount out of anxiety.
🎯
Marketing a Product Nobody Wants
MED RISK
The most painful failure — pouring energy into marketing a design that simply isn't resonating. More posts, better ads, and bigger budgets won't fix a product problem.
Early Warning Signs
No saves on design reveal posts · No comments asking when it drops · Low store session time (people leaving immediately) · Zero DMs about specific designs
✓ Fix: At Month 3, show your 5 designs to 10 people whose taste you respect and ask which one they'd actually pay $65 for. If the answer is "none of them" — iterate before launching more marketing. Product must come before promotion.
📦
The Inventory Cash Trap
MED RISK
Moving to bulk orders too early ties up cash in stock that doesn't move. You can't afford the next drop, Rover income doesn't cover the gap, and the business stalls.
Early Warning Signs
Ordering bulk inventory on a design with fewer than 10 POD sales · Cash drops below 2 months runway after a bulk order · Buying inventory based on optimism rather than sales data
✓ Fix: Never order bulk on a design that hasn't sold at least 10 units on POD. POD is your safety net — use it longer than feels comfortable. The margin improvement from bulk isn't worth the cash risk until demand is proven.
👻
Brand Drift — Losing Your Voice
MED RISK
The content gets more polished, more generic, more brand-like and less personal. The emotional connection erodes. Followers stop caring. Sales slow without an obvious reason.
Early Warning Signs
Scrolling back through your own posts and not feeling anything · No personal founder posts in the last 3 weeks · Captions that could belong to any brand · Mimicking other brands' aesthetics
✓ Fix: SBW wins by being more SBW, not by being less. Once a month write a post that makes you slightly uncomfortable to publish — that vulnerability is exactly what builds loyal customers.
⭐
Quality Problem Left Unaddressed
LOW RISK
One bad quality batch at premium prices generates a cluster of bad reviews that takes months to undo. The risk is low with POD but not zero — especially as you shift to bulk.
Early Warning Signs
A customer mentions the color looks different in person · Print feels rough or cracked · A review mentions sizing running small · You receive a return request
✓ Fix: Order and physically approve every new product before it goes live. Handle every quality complaint immediately and generously — replace it, no questions. One handled complaint can become your best review.
📊
The Comparison Trap
LOW RISK
Pivoting your strategy, pricing, or aesthetic based on what other brands appear to be doing. Social media hides the reality of most small brands. Most that look successful are not.
Early Warning Signs
Changing your aesthetic after seeing a competitor's perform well · Dropping prices because someone else's are lower · Abandoning a direction after one slow week · Strategy changes based on vibes, not your own data
✓ Fix: Your only competitor is last month's SBW. Every decision should be driven by your own Shopify data, your own engagement metrics, and your own customer feedback — not someone else's highlight reel.
