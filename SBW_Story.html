The Starting Point

NO JOB.
NO EXCUSES.

I'm a graphic designer with 8 years of experience. I just got let go. Rent is $1,850 a month. Phone is $150. That's $2,000 I need to find every single month — starting now.

I had two choices: panic, or build. I chose to build. Not because I wasn't scared. Because being broke and building something is still better than being broke and doing nothing.

Day 1 · May 27, 2026

I launched my Rover dog sitting profile, completed 9 Uber Eats deliveries, and made $89.92 gross — all before dinner. It wasn't glamorous. It was proof that action beats anxiety every time.

What I Learned Fast

RULES I
LIVE BY
NOW.

01
ACTION BEFORE PERFECT
The Rover profile didn't need to be perfect to go live. The first Instagram post didn't need to be perfect to get posted. Done beats perfect every time when you're starting from zero.
02
KNOW YOUR NUMBER
My number is $2,000/month. Every decision I make gets filtered through that. Does this move me toward $2,000 or away from it? It's a surprisingly powerful question.
03
USE WHAT YOU HAVE
I have a design background. I have a phone. I have 8 years of creative experience. Most people overlook what they already have because it doesn't feel like enough. It's enough.
04
SEPARATE THE MONEY
Business checking. Tax fund savings. Personal checking. Three accounts, zero confusion. Set aside 25–30% of everything for taxes before you spend a dollar of it.
05
REST IS PRODUCTIVE
A burned-out founder makes bad decisions. The schedule I built has real rest built in — not as a reward, but as infrastructure. You can't build something lasting on empty.
