South Side
Asylum
Michigan Underground — Downriver's Most Notorious Label
Words: Skandal

Throughout my years of collecting there have only been a few times that I've discovered a label with such a diverse catalogue of music that has stayed relatively unknown to collectors and underground music enthusiasts, such as the South Side Asylum.

Founded by Dr Capone after moving from NY to Michigan in the early 90s and holding down the streets of Ecorse, River Rouge, Delray and S/W Detroit, this collective of Downriver emcees included: Dr Capone, Hallow Point aka Lil Weto, Dirty Weto, 24K, Live-1, Da Marshall, UGA, YG's, Reformed Illusionz, Illcawshun Tribe, C4N, ADB, Neromancaz & 6 Point Raiderz.

With limited resources the SSA had minimal networks for distribution and were only available at limited locations around the Downriver area, made in quantities sometimes as low as 20 units.

The SSA were notorious for their gang affiliation which ultimately led to the label being blacklisted by local community groups and councils.

A true testament to an underground culture synonymous with the 90s — a golden era of inspirational and foundational rap for local artists and a movement that has shaped a genre as we know it today.

Field Report
The Memphis
Connection
Recounting buying tapes at Royal City Sounds, TN
Words: Skandal & Wild Bill

I met Bill over a decade ago through collecting and noticed he had some old Memphis tapes and sent him a message. I could tell the guy loved his tapes after we spoke for a while — he recounted the whereabouts of the stores he had bought them from and said they had sentimental value and wasn't sure about selling them, but I was persistent.

I made a significant offer on one specific tape that caught my eye — Lil Gin's The Serpent's Step Son. I had never seen this particular variation at the time. The cover was an early version with a young picture of him posing in what looks like a condemned building. I'd only ever seen the version that MVP had released, which Derrick (Skinny Pimp) told me was a fan-made bootleg.

We came to agreement on the tape and it's been in my collection since. I recently caught up with Bill again when he let me back into his Memphis time capsule.

"It was only side 1! I became obsessed.."

— Wild Bill

"The first thing I ever heard as far as the underground stuff was a kid had a dub of a dub of a dub of side one of Smoked Out, Loced Out on the school bus. I borrowed it and made my own dub."

"I found another kid that had only one half of side 1 of DJ Paul's Greatest Hits. I made a dub of that too. Eventually this older dude I knew traded me his Criminal Manne tape for a MC Breed tape! I begged him to tell me where he got it from. He would only tell me he got it in Jackson."

"A janitor at Burger King told us where Royal City Sounds was — a blessed day for sure"

— Wild Bill

"It was about an hour from my hometown. The shop was in an old ass house across the street from the Oman Arena. It wasn't a place young white kids usually hung out. My friends were into punk and alternative. They were scared of that area of town. I was obsessed with the music, so I didn't give a damn."

Australian Rap's
Generational Growth
Brad Strut & the Archive that Built a Culture
Words: Skandal & Brad Strut

For as long as I can remember I've been a person that likes to understand things thoroughly when I'm passionate about a subject, and the history behind the origins of hip hop in Australia was an education that took some time to piece together.

Being born generations past the loosely documented birth of the culture, so far from its origins in the US.

I recently reached out to Brad Strut discussing his extensive tape archive. I was staying in St Kilda for the weekend and met Brad at the Balaclava train station where we reminisced about the local graffiti history.

The wall adorned a SINCH dedication piece — Brad recalling the day Sinch died while surfing a train just meters from where we stood, and the crew gatherings that once took place here.

As the rain started we moved onto the footpath and grabbed a coffee. With a boot full of tapes we took cover in an underground car park and started sorting through large plastic tubs containing years of audio: radio dubs, sound desk recordings, demos and freestyle sessions.

I would return to ADL with around 30 tapes to digitize, arrange and catalogue.

Since first hearing Australian rap the thing that appealed to me most was what defined us — the accent, the Aussie content, and especially the locations mentioned in the lyrics.

As I listened to Brad's tapes and chronologically put them in order, it occurred to me that not only was I listening to the history of one of Australia's most relevant emcees — but I was following the footsteps of the earliest pioneers as the music evolved.

From early sessions as JAHD with FRENZIE, drunk freestyle nights with SHAZLEK ONE at OB's Records, to live shows and the music that would lead to the formation of OBESE Records.

Inktown's Finest
SWIGZ
aka Slim Koroma
Detroit / Inkster, MI

In late 1999 Slim Koroma of the group Grim Reality released his solo independent tape Poems Of Life — featuring local Michigan artists Mr Rain, Blue Eyed Devil, Cue Ball & J Bone. The start of Slim's independent career, two and a half decades later it remains a local Inkster classic.

It's 2025 and SLIM KOROMA aka SWIGZ is back with a slammin' new single called "THE GLOVE" — filmed by Envysion Entertainment, recorded at Sound Smith Studios and engineered by Tone Rizzo.

"The Glove" — a pun on Michigan's nickname, The Murder Mitten

SWIGZ takes you on an audio and visual journey through a day in his life cruising the streets of S/W Detroit. You can't help but feel transported — capturing the local vibe perfectly.

Michigan Rap Tape Preservation
Funky Fresh
In Tha Flesh
Skandal  ·  DJ Butter  ·  B-Boy Reg

Every year Detroit celebrates Dilla Month — the month of Jay Dee's birth and death. To mark the occasion, I reached out to DJ Butter and suggested creating a mixtape comprised from selected local Michigan Underground tapes from my archive.

We created the mix together, paired with original artwork by DJ Butter of the late emcee PROOF (D-12, 5 Ela). The host of Funky Fresh In Tha Flesh w/ B-Boy Reg, DJ Butter interviewed me about the project on his magazine-turned-podcast, where we further discussed tapes and Michigan hip hop.

Dedication

"This project was dedicated to JAY DEE, PROOF, BUGZ & all the ones that aren't here — that forged the foundation of hip hop as we know it today."
