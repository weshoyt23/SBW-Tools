WHAT THE NUMBERS MEAN

If 1,000 people see an SBW post this month →
~400 follow or save it →
~150 visit the Shopify store →
~50 add something to cart →
~20 complete a purchase →
~5–8 become repeat buyers who refer others.

This is why reach matters at the top and conversion matters at the bottom.

Stage 01 · Top of Funnel
Awareness
They've never heard of SBW Co. They stumble across a Reel, see a hashtag post, or hear about it from someone in Austin. The goal is simple: stop the scroll.
What Drives Awareness for SBW
🎬
Reels with high production value — cinematic, well-lit, Austin aesthetic. The algorithm pushes Reels to non-followers. This is your free advertising.
📍
Austin location tags on every post — geo-tagged content shows up in Austin searches and gets local traction. Every post, every story, every Reel.
#️⃣
Hashtag strategy — rotating sets of 5–10 relevant hashtags per post. Mix of small (#AustinStreetWear, ~50K), mid (#StreetWearBrand, ~500K), and large (#Streetwear, ~5M).
🤝
Austin creator engagement — genuinely comment on posts from Austin-based creators, brands, and events. Real engagement builds awareness organically.
Metrics to Watch
Reach
Unique accounts who saw any post
Impressions
Total times content was seen
⚠️ Common Leak
Posting inconsistently kills awareness. The algorithm stops showing your content to new people if you go quiet. Even one post per day keeps the pipeline full.
Stage 02 · Upper-Mid Funnel
Interest
They followed you, saved a post, or watched your Reel twice. They're curious but not committed. Your content, your voice, and your story are what keep them here.
What Builds Interest for SBW
🎨
Design process content — showing the work builds credibility and curiosity simultaneously.
🖤
Founder story posts — people follow people, not logos. Wesley building SBW from scratch in Austin is a compelling story. Tell it repeatedly in different ways.
💬
Engaging captions — long, honest captions that make people feel something keep followers from unfollowing.
🗳️
Polls and questions — colorway votes, design feedback, brand decisions made publicly. Participation creates investment in the brand's success.
Metrics to Watch
Saves
Strongest interest signal on IG
Story Views
Your warmest audience size
⚠️ Common Leak
Posting only product content without personality. Balance brand posts with founder posts.
✓ The Fix
For every 2 product/brand posts, post 1 personal founder post. This ratio keeps the brand human and builds emotional connection.
Stage 03 · Mid Funnel
Consideration
They tapped your link in bio and landed on the Shopify store. They're looking at product photos, reading descriptions, checking prices. This is where most small brands lose people.
What Your Shopify Store Must Have
📸
Multiple product photos — front, back, close-up of graphic, lifestyle shot, flat lay. People need to see every angle before spending $65+.
✍️
Compelling descriptions — not specs. Tell the story of the design. What inspired it. What it feels like. Emotion sells.
⭐
Reviews visible — even 3–5 reviews dramatically increase conversion. Install Judge.me (free Shopify app).
🔒
Trust signals — secure checkout badge, clear contact info, a real About page with Wesley's face and story.
📱
Mobile-optimized — test every page on mobile before launch. Almost every SBW customer will shop on their phone.
⚠️ The Biggest Leak
A beautiful Instagram driving traffic to a generic, unfinished Shopify store. The store must match the brand's quality.
✓ The Fix
Spend as much time on your Shopify store as you do on your Instagram. Every product page is a landing page. Treat it like one.
Stage 04 · Lower-Mid Funnel
Intent
They added to cart. This is a very strong signal. But cart abandonment rates average 70%. Your job here is to close the gap between wanting and buying.
What Converts Intent to Purchase
⚡
Frictionless checkout — enable Shop Pay and Apple Pay. One-tap checkout converts dramatically better than multi-step forms.
📧
Abandoned cart email — a simple "you left something behind" email with a product photo recovers 5–15% of abandoned carts.
⏰
Honest urgency — "Only 8 left in this size" or "Drop 1 closes [date]" creates real urgency when true. Never fake scarcity.
🎁
Free shipping threshold — "Free shipping over $80" nudges people to add a second item. Increases AOV and reduces abandonment.
Metrics to Watch
Cart Rate
% of store visitors who add to cart
Abandonment
% who add to cart but don't buy
⚠️ Common Leak
No email collection means abandoned carts are gone forever. Set up Klaviyo from day one.
Stage 05 · Conversion
Purchase
They bought. This is the moment everything has been building toward — and it's also the start of the next phase. How you treat them now determines if they ever come back.
What Makes the First Purchase Unforgettable
📦
Packaging that surprises — tissue paper, a branded sticker, a handwritten thank-you card from Wesley. At $65–$110, people expect premium. Deliver it.
✉️
Personal order confirmation — not a generic Shopify email. A message in SBW's voice that makes them feel like they're part of something.
📲
Tag request card — "Tag us on Instagram @sbwco for a chance to be featured." This turns customers into content creators and awareness drivers.
Metrics to Watch
Conv. Rate
% of store visitors who purchase
AOV
Average order value
Stage 06 · Post-Purchase
Loyalty
They bought once. Your goal is to make them buy again and bring their friends. Repeat customers are 5x cheaper to convert than new ones.
What Builds Loyalty at SBW
💌
Personal DM to every first-time buyer — "Hey [name], thank you so much — it means everything." This scales until you have too many orders for it not to.
📸
Repost every tag — when a customer posts wearing SBW, repost it to your story immediately. This rewards buyers and signals to followers that real people love the brand.
🔑
Drop 2 early access — email past buyers first. "You were one of our first — here's early access before the public drop." This creates an inner circle.
⭐
Review request email — 7 days post-delivery. "How does it feel?" with a direct link to leave a review. Reviews drive the next wave of consideration.
Metrics to Watch
Repeat Rate
% who buy more than once
Review Rate
% who leave a review
EMAIL SEQUENCE — DROP 1 LAUNCH

6 emails. Start building this sequence in Klaviyo before launch. Each email has one job.

E1 Welcome to SBW Co. On signup ▼
Goal

Set expectations and establish the brand voice. This is their first impression of SBW outside of Instagram. Make it feel personal — not corporate.

Key Elements

Wesley's photo + a short founder note. What SBW is and why it exists. What they can expect from these emails (drop announcements, behind-the-scenes, early access). Link to Instagram.

E2 The Story Behind the Brand Day 3 ▼
Goal

Deepen the connection. People buy from people they believe in. This email is where Wesley's story does the selling.

Key Elements

The gap in the market SBW fills. What "premium graphic apparel" means at SBW vs. fast fashion. The design philosophy. A preview of the design process — sketch photo or in-progress shot.

E3 Drop 1 Is Coming — Here's a Preview 1 week before drop ▼
Goal

Build anticipation. This is the teaser. Show enough to excite, hide enough to compel them to come back on drop day.

Key Elements

A cropped or partially revealed product image. The drop date and time. A note about limited availability (if true). "You're getting this first because you're on the list."

E4 Drop 1 Is LIVE — Shop Now Drop day, 9am ▼
Goal

Drive immediate traffic to the store. This email has one call to action: shop now. No distractions.

Key Elements

Full product reveal — hero image front and center. Product names, prices, and a direct Shop Now button. One sentence from Wesley about this drop. Urgency if applicable.

E5 You Left Something Behind Abandoned cart, 2hr ▼
Goal

Recover the abandoned cart. This is automated in Klaviyo. It should feel personal — not like a system email.

Key Elements

Photo of the exact item left in cart. A short, friendly note: "Still thinking it over? No pressure." Direct checkout link. If stock is actually low: "Only X left in your size."

E6 How Does It Feel? 7 days post-delivery ▼
Goal

Collect a review and plant the seed for the next purchase. This email is low-pressure and builds relationship capital.

Key Elements

A warm check-in from Wesley. Direct review link (Judge.me or Google). An ask to tag @sbwco on Instagram with their photo. A brief tease: "Drop 2 is already in the works — stay tuned."
